Project Outline

  • Next

As we have slowly incorporated the internet into our daily lives, a necessity for many, the way in which we navigate adapts and changes all the time. Currently, a popular way to navigate the Internet, be it on a Computer, Cell phone, or speaker is to use an assistant. Google Assistant, Alexa, Siri, Bixby and Cortana are all popular picks for device assistants, they are extremely useful and convenient in navigating internet connected devices, hands-free. While hands-free is extremely convenient, I find that I am talking to a voice without a face and personality.

ANPC Desktop Companion bridges this gap, making voice and text commands more fun and interactive with a cute anime character of your choosing running around and doing tasks for you on your desktop and in your browser. ANPC will do all the same things that a normal voice/text assistant can do, but with added personality and flair, running from program to program to open them for you on a click, playing a song at your request or simply sitting idle on your desktop eating a bowl of noodles. ANPC aims to put not only a name, but a face and unique personality to aid you in your daily tasks, while also keeping you and your desktop company.

Project Inspiration

  • Next

I was inspired by the a few things, the first being the Desktop Goose by samperson, where a goose roams your desktop and does generally unhelpful things, like tracking mud on your screen, stealing your cursor or bringing "gifts", namely goose related images/memes onto your screen. I thought that this was unique and charming, and wanted to incorperate an action-response into my design.

My second source of inspiration was, as stated in the project outline, IVA's, or voice assistants. I find myself regularly using mine - several times a day. Whether im cooking, working on a project or simply too lazy to type, my Google Assistant is always ready to answer my questions, play a song or open an app. I wanted to implement something that could interperate human speech and text and respond not only by voice or type, but actions too.

Digging Deeper

  • Next

Through some research, I found that the idea of a desktop companion has already been done, but they were all for fun and visual aesthetic, whereas mine would be more functional as it primary purpose is an intelligent personal assistant (IPA) . The few i found that inspired me were The Ghost on the Desktop and Desktop Kanjo. Both of these are desktop companion/mascots, designed to acompany you when doing work by sitting on your desktop, so there is opporunity for one that is not only cute, but functional too.

Audience & Visual Communication

  • Next

ANPC is aimed at users who love or share an interest in anime, games, Japanese culture or those who regularly use voice assistants. It is a very niche concept, so the targeted age-group would be teens and young adults as they are generally more exposed to the internet, and in turn more likely to have an interest in anime.

The Visual Communication has been designed to appeal to an audience which watches anime, plays games, is interested in Japanese culture, or just likes cute things. I have combined a cool Japanese-looking font, Gang of Three, and contrasted it with a slim sans-serif font, Big Noodle Tilting, that draws the eye to first the bigger text which will capture attention, and then drawing it over to the sub text. I have incorporated the mascot into the logo to captivate users who may be interested in anime. The colours I have chosen are warm and friendly delivering a comforting and sweet aesthetic, while also matching the mascot.

Colour and Font choice

I Developed a logo first of all and made iterations of it that would be suitable as a webpage header, a desktop icon and the name of the applicaton.

I chose to put the mascot in the logo because I thought it would easily attract my audience, and is relevant to the topic of the application so not is not misleading.
